Cocktails of the Clubhouse

House Favorites

Amaretto Whiskey Sour

Scotch. Disaronno. Lemon. Sugar.

A combination that may sound strange at first, we're confident that this mix of almond sweetness, citrus, and whiskey is something everyone should try.

Cuban Missile Crisis

Rumchata. Kahlua. Godiva liqueur.

This playful take on a White Russian, thought up in Milwaukee, WI, has become a go-to after-dinner classic.

Blue Drink

Bacardi Coconut. Pineapple. Cream of Coconut. Blue Curacao.

If you like Pina Coladas, and getting caught in the rain, and drinks that are blue.

Sneaky Pete

Jaegermeister. Disaronno. Amaro Ramazotti. Coke.

Don't let the Jaeger scare you. This coca-cola based drink is sweet, uniquely complex, and a surprising crowd-pleaser.


Classics

Sazerac

Rye Whiskey. Herbsaint. Peychaud's Bitters. Sugar.

Some call it the original cocktail. A New Orleans staple, made many ways over the years, we recreate this drink with the absinthe-like Herbsaint, in the fashion of the aptly-named Sazerac Bar in New Orleans.

Whiskey Sour

Bourbon. Lemon. Sugar.

Strong, sour, and just the right amount of sweet. This is how we like ours.

Vieux Carre

Brandy. Rye. Sweet Vermouth. Benedictine. Peychaud's Bitters. Angostura Bitters.

Translating to "Old Quarter", this referrs to the French Quarter of New Orleans. A lesser-known classic, it's a strong but surprisingly balanced and complex drink.

Rum and Coke

Rum. Coke.

If you add a lime wedge, you call it a 'Cubalibre'. Here's to a free Cuba.

Ramos Gin Fizz

Gin. Lemon-Lime Sour. Cream. Egg White. Fizz.

This creamy yet refreshing drink is a unique but classic cocktail. It's a bit labor-intensive, and at the peak of its 20th-century popularity dozens of bartenders could be found shaking this drink in relays in New Orleans bars.
